Something I found interesting about high school is the increased amount of contact post school hours that I have with my classmates. Night Before Pre-ACT
Tuesday April 8, 2025.

Ninth grade feels too early to be taking a pre-ACT test, but at least we get the pre-ACT. I spent the most normal 8 hours at school, and then started reviewing test taking strategies, like elimination, picking a random letter to put for questions I don’t know, and pacing myself. I find another strategy to be really good - looking back at the test as soon as you are done. My evening was rather uneventful; I emptied my chromebook case of everything except pencils, pens, an eraser, my chromebook charger, my calculators (finally I can use my graphing calculator!), and my wireless mouse. I did the little homework I had the motivation to do, and slept early.

I ended up not taking any pictures at all today because I was so inccredibly tired and slept at 7:30pm. Beware, I won't be able to sleep before 10 any other night in this blog.

Pre-ACT Day
Wednesday April 9, 2025.

The agenda for the day: survive the Pre-ACT, do my best, and go HOME. In the midst of the Pre-ACT, I had entirely forgotten our preview concert for band tonight. The Pre-ACT wasn’t bad at all. I particularly enjoyed the math section, even though I know it’s the most commonly hated subject. I know for sure I’ll have to improve my reading skills, but I have time. I ended my night with a movie, and proceeded to fall asleep halfway through Ursula's death.

Today was the busiest single day of school I have had in a long, long time. School went well, and I skipped eighth hour math to travel to Williamsville with the choirs. The tasks for the day were:
Work on this 20Time project,
Study for an English Quiz,
Read Section 2, Chapter 5 of 1984,

Driver’s Ed Outline
