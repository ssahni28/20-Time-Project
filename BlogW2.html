Study Day
Wednesday April 16, 2025.

Today was just the kind of day where you have no extracurricular activities, but you’re still up til 1am studying. I even got home an hour early, but any amount of time isn’t enough for a bio test. My personal favorite method of studying for bio tests is pretty simple, but unfortunately, incredibly time-consuming. The night before the quiz, I review all my notes and hand-write an outline as though I were preparing a lecture or a worksheet. I use that to review concepts and definitions. I also draw the diagrams out the night before the quiz to help me remember the placement of all the organs in the body systems so that I have an extra day to review them. The night before the test, (so the day I take the quiz), I go home and immediately review all my notes and worksheets. I then finish up all my homework, eat dinner, wind down, and go back to my outline. I fill in all the information I can think of off the top of my head, and go back in later to fill in the gaps. This technique, called assisted blurting, is a form of assisted active recall that really helps me see where I have gaps in my knowledge, prepare for assessments, and have useful study tools for final exams.
